The Huntarian Museum

Scotlands Oldest Museum

History

The Hunterian Museum was founded in 1807, it is one of Scotland’s oldest public museums and was once a university. Later after opening the University moved to a new site in Gilmorehill as the city centre was too crowded. The money in order to build the museum came from a Scottish scientist by the name of “William Hunter”. Hunter was an avid collector of all kinds of European art, from coins, minerals and paintings to even collecting insects. The Hunterian has closed once for additions but it re-opened in September 2011 with a new gallery dedicated to Romans in Scotland

Collection

The collections of the museum are contained within multiple different buildings around it’s campus. Those buildings are: The Hunterian Museum, Zoology Museum, Hunterian Art Gallery and The Mackintosh House. The first building, the Hunterian Museum, contains items such as scientific instruments, items on the Roman Empire, and some also from ancient Egypt. The second building, The Zoology Museum, contains an extensive insect collection including some items from William Hunter. The third building, The Hunterian Art Gallery, houses an extensive collection on general and watercolour art. It also has a garden contain many different sculptures. The final building, The Mackintosh House, is a recreation of the house which the architect “Charles Rennie Mackintosh” lived in. It features very extensive woodwork like that seen in Mackintosh’s real home.

Contributors

William Hunter

One of the main contributors was the Scottish scientist “William Hunter”. Who, along with being a physician, was also a leading teacher in anatomy. One collection he is well known for is the “Hunter Coin Cabinet” with is on display in the Hunterian museum and contains a variety of different coins from around the world.

James McNeill Whistler

Another major contributor is “James McNeill Whistler” an American artist and painter. A painting he is well known for is the “Symphony in White, No. 1: The White Girl” a full body portrait of a woman clad in a white dress, it is currently contained in the National Gallery of Art in Washington D.C.
